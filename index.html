Day 1 (2016/07/23)

AJATT immersion!!!

I'm going to start doing AJATT-style immersion in Japanese because I'm tired of sucking at Japanese! This sucking needs to stop! I had a few false starts in the past, but after reading AJATT, I know I'll be motivated to follow through this time for a few reasons:

  1. I know I can do this.
  2. I know if I do this it will work and I'll be fluent.
  3. I know that it will be worth it.

Won't immersion be really hard??

All I have to do is show up, so no. The hardest part will probably be just getting in the habit of showing up.

100% OF WINNING IS SHOWING UP

Khatz (AJATT)
Easy parts:
- Since I'm already familiar with Chinese characters, the kanji will be the easiest part to learn.
Not as easy parts (initially):
- Getting in the habit to playing Japanese all the time.
- Getting in the habit of making new Anki cards every day.
- The first 1000 Japanese-Japanese Anki cards.

Every possible second of time will be in Japanese!!! 

        while (isAlive) {
          listenTo('Japanese stuff');
          read('Japanese stuff');
        }

I'm going to go at this pretty hard because I want to be fluent as quickly as possible. I imagine that after getting in the habit of doing this every day, it will get a lot easier.

The first month will definitely be the most difficult.

Will this even work??? Don't you need Genki/textbooks/grammar books/X???

Only time will tell. I think it's just a matter of clocking in my 10,000 listening hours and putting in my 10,000 J-J Anki cards.

The beginning plan (first month):

  • Learn 100 Glossika Japanese sentences a day (to get a good base of 3000 sentences with audio)
  • Learn 100 Kanji from Remembering the Kanji a day (to be able to read all the Kanji I'll ever come across)
  • Listen to Japanese as much as possible (to get used to Japanese's sounds)
  • Read as much Japanese as possible (get used to reading and adding sentences)

This is the beginning of something awesome

I'll be tracking my progress and continuing to share my thoughts.
